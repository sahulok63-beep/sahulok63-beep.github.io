Most ideas don't survive contact with a direct question. I write to find out which ones do.

This is a working notebook on philosophy and psychology — Advaita next to Stoicism, Camus next to the Dhammapada, tested for consistency rather than taken on faith. No gurus, no final answers, just thinking out loud.

About

I'm Lok. I read philosophy — Indian and Western — not as belief systems to adopt but as tools to test against each other and against ordinary life. Most of what I write here starts as a question I couldn't drop.

I'm not interested in performing certainty. If a tradition's claim doesn't hold up when you push on it, I say so — whether that tradition is Vedanta, Stoicism, or whatever the loudest voice online is selling this week.
